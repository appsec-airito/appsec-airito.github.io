Security is a field that rewards clear thinking under uncertainty. The people who move it
forward aren't the ones with the longest tool lists — they're the ones who can reason about
risk, communicate it to people who don't share their background, and make a decision when the
data is incomplete.

This space is where I'll work through those ideas in public.

## What you'll find here

I want to write about the parts of security that don't fit neatly into a scanner report:

- **How to think about risk** when the threat is novel and the budget is fixed.
- **Where engineering and security meet** — building systems that are secure by design rather
  than patched after the fact.
- **Leadership in security** — turning a function that's often seen as a blocker into one that
  makes the whole organization faster and more resilient.

## Why in public

Writing forces precision. If I can't explain an idea clearly, I probably don't understand it as
well as I thought. Publishing raises the bar further — and if the ideas are useful to someone
else facing the same problems, even better.
